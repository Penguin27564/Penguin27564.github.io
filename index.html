Healthy Eating and Nutrition

Healthy eating habits include replacing foods that contain trans fats, added salt, and sugar with more nutritious options. Following a healthy diet has many health benefits, such as:
  • Strengthing your bones and teeth
  • Reduces your risk of heart disease and type 2 diabetes
  • Provides the energy you need to keep active
  • Prevent diet-related illnesses, like some cancers
  • Maintaining a healthy weight
A healthy diet typically includes nutrient-dense foods from all of the major food groups, such as:
  • Lean proteins
  • Whole grains
  • Healthy fats
  • Fruits and Vegetables

The Healthy Plate

Quarter plate of Wholegrains

Wholegrains are rich in nutrients due to them not being over-processed.
Refined grains such as white rice and white bread have been processed which results in the loss of valuable nutrients.

What do wholegrains provide?

Wholegrains contain vitamins B and E, minerals such as iron, zinc and magnesium, phytochemicals and dietary fibre.

What are the benefits of eating wholegrains?

Eating wholegrains can help you with weight management by keeping you feeling full for longer, which helps prevent overeating.
Having wholegrains as part of your meal plan can also help lower your risk of developing chronic diseases such as heart diseases, diabetes and certain cancers in the long run.

Quarter plate of protein

Proteins are a vital part of a health diet. They are made up of chemical building blocks called amino acids. Your body uses these amino acids to build and repair muscles and bones and as an energy source. Examples of protein are:
  • Poultry
  • Red Meat
  • Seafood
  • Eggs
  • Dairy Products (eg: Milk, Yogurt, Cheese)
  • Plant based protein (eg: Soy Milk, Tofu, Nuts)

What do proteins provide?

Proteins contain vitamins and minerals such as
  • Vitamin B
  • Zinc
  • Iron

What are the benefits of eating protein?

Proteins help to build bones, muscles, cartilage, and skin. Your hair and skin are comprised mostly of protein. Proteins also help to repair tissue
Your red blood cells contain a protein compound which carries oxygen throughout the body, supplying the body with the neccessary nutrients.

Half a plate of fruits and vegetables

Fruit and vegetables are part of a healthy, balanced diet. Evidence shows there are significant health benefits to getting at least 5 portions of fruit and vegetables total, not 5 portions of each. One portion weighs about 80 grams.

What do fruits and vegetables provide?

Fruits and vegetables are low calorie foods that are rich in dietary fibre, vitamins, minerals, phytochemicals, and antioxidants.

What are the benefits of eating fruits and vegetables?

A diet rich in fruit and vegetables helps protect against heart disease, stroke, and event certain cancers. The high water and fibre content also minimises urges of overeating.
